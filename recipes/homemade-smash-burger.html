Homemade Smash Burger

Description

This smash burger recipe makes super juicy burgers with crispy edges. Don't use meat leaner than 80/20, and make sure meat is very cold. Prefer cooking outdoors to avoid smoking kitchen. They grill fast so make sure everything is ready to go.

Ingredients

  • 4 hamburger buns
  • 2 tablespoons butter, softened
  • 1 pound ground chuck beef (80% lean)
  • 4 6-inch square parchment paper
  • salt
  • 4 slices American cheese
  • burger toppings of choice

Steps

  1. Preheat an outdoor grill for high heat and lightly oil the grate. Set a cast iron flat-top griddle or large cast iron skillet onto the grill and preheat until smoking.
  2. Spread butter on the inside of the buns and toast on the flat-top until lightly browned. Set aside.
  3. Form meat into 8 loosely-packed balls, 2 ounces each. Do not pack the meat tightly, as this will prevent it from smashing properly. Place each ball on the hot flat-top, cover with a piece of parchment and immediately smash down to a 1/4 inch thickness using 2 stuff, sturdy spatulas that are criss-crossed to get propper leverage as you press down. You may also use the bottom of small skillet. Sprinkle the meat with salt/
  4. Grill for about 45 seconds, until the edges are dark brown and the centers are a light pink color. Using a bench scraper or firm spatula, gently scrape up the patties, flip over and immediately cover 4 of them with cheese. Grill an additional 15 to 20 seconds; stack the plain patties over the cheese-covered patties so you have 4 stacks. Move each stack to a bun and serve with your favorite toppings.
